        Case Study / Highlight Industries
        
E-commerce: Shopify

        
E-commerce. Website Design. UX. Shopify. GitHub. Highlight Industries.


        
Highlight Industries is a packaging machinery manufacturer specializing in high-end custom stretch wrapping and packaging solutions. In addition to designing and selling specialized machines, the company supports long-term customer needs through a Shopify parts store, giving customers a faster, easier way to order replacement parts, accessories, and essential components.


        
Goal

        
Highlight Industries needed its Shopify store to better support industrial customers while also reducing shipping-related losses caused by inaccurate product weights, unclear box sizing, and incomplete shipping data. Many products lacked consistent visuals, categories, tags, and shipping details, which made the site harder to shop and created costly UPS billing discrepancies when Shopify did not charge enough to cover actual shipping costs.


        
Creative direction

        
To improve the store, I photographed products in the warehouse and edited the images for Shopify, creating clearer visuals for customers ordering replacement parts online. I also led a team project to weigh products, identify accurate shipping box sizes, and update product data so Shopify shipping rates more closely reflected actual UPS costs. Alongside the shipping improvements, I cleaned up product categories and tags, clarified shipping and return language, and helped organize the site around how industrial customers search for needed parts. I also redesigned a landing page that felt custom, while still relying on Shopify’s native navigation to keep a smooth connection between the custom landing page and the shop.


        
Outcome

        
These updates created a clearer, more reliable Shopify experience for both customers and internal teams. The improved product organization, visuals, shipping clarity, and service pathways supported measurable growth. Operationally, the updated weight and box-size process helped reduce UPS billing discrepancies by approximately 60%, from around 10–15 discrepancies per billing cycle down to 3–5.


        

          113% Total Sales
          118% Gross Sales
          125% Net Sales
          Plus approximately 60% fewer UPS billing discrepancies.

        Case Study / Highlight Industries
        
Technical Writing: Quick Start Guides

        
Technical Writing. Highlight Industries. Process Improvement.

        
Highlight Industries needed a more efficient way to get customers the right startup information without relying on bulky printed manuals for every machine. The work became both a technical writing project and a process improvement project: simplify the information, reduce unnecessary printing, and make manuals easier to access digitally.

        
Goal

        
Reduce labor, ink, and paper costs while improving the customer experience. SEO research showed that customers and manufacturing workers were looking for machine manuals and startup information, so the goal was to create a clearer path to quick setup guidance and full digital manuals.

        
Creative direction

        
I condensed 100+ page machine manuals into smaller quick start guides that could stay with the machine. The guides used cleaner images, shorter instructions, and more direct language so customers could understand key setup and operation steps right off the pallet.

        
Outcome

        
The quick start guides included QR codes that connected customers to digital manuals online. Those manuals were uploaded through GitHub, and the Support Center was designed to help customers find the correct manual by sales order or machine serial number. This created a more practical customer handoff while supporting internal efficiency and reducing unnecessary printed materials.

        

          100+ Page Manuals Condensed
          QR Access to Digital Manuals
          GitHub + Support Center Workflow
          Technical writing used as process improvement for customer support, manufacturing handoff, and manual access.

        Case Study / Kenowa Hills Public Schools
        
Immigration Support Handbook

        
A time-sensitive internal resource created to help staff use district-approved language and provide consistent support to immigrant students and families.


        
Goal

        
Create one document that housed important information, interaction guidelines, and resources so staff across Kenowa Hills Public Schools could respond consistently and provide families with the same approved information.


        
Creative direction

        
Develop an easy-to-navigate handbook that could support front-office staff, administrators, counselors, students, and families. The project was expedited following a school incident and an emergency district-level meeting.


        
Outcome

        
I researched resources and handbooks from other school districts, organized the findings in a shared drive for team collaboration, and created standardized guidance for staff. The handbook included procedures for front-office interactions, district contacts, and resources that counselors and administrators could provide to students and families.

        Case Study / Kenowa Hills Public Schools
        
Process Improvement: PBIS

        
Kenowa Hills Public Schools. Education. Process Improvement. Research. Graphic Design. Surveys. Community.


        
Kenowa Hills Middle School needed a Tier 1 PBIS program that felt clearer, more memorable, and more meaningful for both staff and students. The goal was not just to redesign materials, but to build a system people would actually use and recognize as their own.


        
Goal

        
Strengthen fidelity around PBIS Tier 1 programming by simplifying the schoolwide expectations into a shorter, more usable framework, while increasing staff and student involvement across the school community.


        
Creative direction

        
I approached the project like a process-improvement and communication problem. The work started with PBIS research, interviews with local schools and PBIS coordinators, and input gathered through surveys, small groups, and collaboration with the Tier 1 team. From there, I helped shape a program that connected strategy, language, and visuals, then created educational pieces for students and families and reached out to local businesses to help support participation.


        
Outcome

        
The result was the GOLD campaign: Grit, On Task, Leadership, and Dependable. The new framework gave the school a simpler set of expectations and a more cohesive identity to build around. Participation increased from roughly 30% to 90%, staff and student involvement increased by 60% across more than 80 staff members and 600 students, and the work helped strengthen school culture while reconnecting the program to the surrounding community.


        

          30% → 90% Participation
          +60% Staff + Student Involvement
          80+ Staff · 600+ Students
          Research-driven process improvement supported by communication design, surveys, and community partnership.

        Case Study / Highlight Industries
        
Digital Business Cards

        
A branded contact-sharing system that evolved from static email signatures into a mobile-first experience for employees, customers, and trade shows.


        
Goal

        
Create a more professional and consistent way for employees to share contact information across email, mobile devices, QR codes, and in-person events.


        
Creative direction

        
The project started with branded email signature concepts, then expanded into a responsive digital business card. I designed the system to work on desktop and mobile, with clear contact actions, QR sharing, save-to-contacts tools, and employee information managed through a CSV file.


        
Process improvement

        
Instead of creating and updating individual cards one at a time, the shared layout could pull employee details from structured data. That made it easier to keep names, titles, phone numbers, photos, and booth information consistent while giving employees one reusable link to share.


        
Outcome

        
The final system created a cleaner brand experience and a more practical way to exchange contact information. Employees could share a QR code, send the card to a phone, or let customers save contact details directly without relying on printed cards or outdated signatures.
